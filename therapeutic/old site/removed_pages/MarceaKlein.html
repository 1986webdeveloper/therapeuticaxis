Marcea Klein

Counselling with a Buddhist Flavour
“The moment we admit we are in fact responsible for ourselves, we open to the possibility of change”
We all long for something or someway to take us outside ourselves and avoid the experience of life’s unpredictable transitions; relationships, separation, divorce, marriage, career, re-locating, children leaving home, menopause, sickness, old age and death.
Buddhist Counselling works from a compassionate point of view and recognises that these transitions in life inter-act with our own feelings of : low self-esteem, inadequacy, anxiety, lack of recognition, lack of feeling loved or understood, or a deep sense of powerless and valuelessness.
By letting go of the endless desires, cravings, attachment and aversion and bringing them into more self-awareness one can live a purposeful life that transcends our personal identity.  We are the only beings who can change our lives through changing intentions and actions.

Sometimes it is just as simple as giving yourself the time and space to be heard in a safe, supportive environment and explore beliefs, assumptions and habits that may or may not be of benefit any longer.
In all cultures, times and places people have dreamt.  Dreaming can be a very powerful tool in understanding oneself more deeply and achieving a more awakened state of being.  Dreams can also assist the therapeutic process by gaining more insight into our core wounds.
Each person’s experience of therapy is different, and the benefits also depend on a vital, engaging, nurturing and compassionate relationship between client and therapist.   I am passionate about people’s willingness and capacity for transformation through conscious awareness, which ultimately can lead to more intimacy, warm heartedness, inner strength, resilience, clarity and authenticity.
…”Instead of always looking somewhere else for what is precious, realize it is you who are precious, realize it is you. You are the jewel.”
About Marcea Klein, Clinical Psychotherapist, Buddhist Counsellor and Psychotherapist, Post Grad. Dream Analyst
After establishing and directing the East West Centres and Wholefood Cooking Schools in Sydney and London, working as a Journalist and publishing 7 books on Health and Healing and studying Tibetan Buddhism for 20 years, Marcea began to develop an interest in weaving together the body, mind and psyche connection.  Over the past 25 years she has worked as aCounsellor, Psychotherapist, and Nutritional Counsellor developing her own method of health and healing that impacts on physical, psychological and emotional well being.

  Benefits of Buddhist Counselling

  • Creating more harmonious, intimate, balanced relationships
  • Expanding and deepening one’s understanding of self
  • New skills to manage, depression, stress and anxiety
  • Building inner strength, resilience & flexibility
  • Working with dreams
  • Finding Meaning, Passion, Connection and Purpose in life
  • Opening to new aspects of self through richer, more meaningful communication
  • Meditation

Marcea Klein is a member of the Institute Of Clinical Psychotherapy (ICP), The Council for Responsible Nutrition, and Psychotherapy and Counselling Association of Australia. (PACFA)
